Technology

Your hand becomes the interface — computed locally, framed like jewellery.

Hand landmarks

MediaPipe Hands

A lightweight model tracks your hand in the browser. Landmarks map to cursor position, then stable poses trigger navigation — swipes from wrist motion, pinch from thumb and index proximity.

Privacy first

Frames never leave your machine unless you choose to share them. The camera feeds the model only; nothing is uploaded for this experience.

Designed calm

Cooldowns and smoothing keep motion from feeling jittery. The HUD mirrors what the system believes you are doing, so the interface stays legible.

Story

Three chapters — scroll or gesture through the arc of the house.

I

The atelier

We began in a small workshop where pattern pieces outnumbered chairs. Every garment was cut twice — once in paper, once in cloth — so the drape could be argued in daylight before a single stitch.

II

The silence of material

Wool from the highlands, silk from river valleys, leather from tanneries that still work by hand. We choose fibres that age with dignity: creases that look earned, not careless.

III

The room you enter

Maison Elan is not only what you wear but how you move through a space. This site is an experiment in that same restraint — gesture as invitation, not command.

About this experience

Gesture interaction asks for a different kind of attention. Without a mouse, you negotiate distance and timing — the interface becomes theatrical, almost intimate.

We built this layer on top of a classic editorial layout so luxury codes stay intact: serif headlines, generous margins, and gold used as punctuation, not noise.

“Technology is at its best when it disappears into ritual.”

If gestures feel unfamiliar at first, allow a few passes. The engine learns your scale of motion within the session.
